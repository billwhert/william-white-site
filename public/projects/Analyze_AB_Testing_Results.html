Part III - Experimentation

1. If you want to assume that the control page is better unless the treatment page proves to be definitely better at a Type I error rate of 5%, you state your null and alternative hypotheses in terms of $p_{control}$ and $p_{treatment}$ as:

$H_{0}: p_{control} >= p_{treatment}$

$H_{1}: p_{control} < p_{treatment}$

Which is equivalent to:

$H_{0}: p_{treatment} - p_{control} <= 0$

$H_{1}: p_{treatment} - p_{control} > 0$

Assume under the null hypothesis, $p_{treatment}$ and $p_{control}$ both have "true" success rates equal to the converted success rate regardless of page - that is $p_{treatment}$ and $p_{control}$ are equal. Furthermore, assume they are equal to the converted rate in df regardless of the page.

a) The convert rate for $p_{treatment}$ under the null. The convert rate for $p_{control}$ under the null. The sample size for the control and the sample size for the treatment are from the original dataset.

e) Simulate 500 $p_{treatment}$ - $p_{control}$ values using this same process as b)- d). Store all 500 values in an numpy array called p_diffs.

In[107]:
p_diffs = []
for _ in range(500):
    treatment_converted_r = np.random.binomial(n=1, p=p_control_treatment_null, size=n_treatment)
    control_converted_r = np.random.binomial(n=1, p=p_control_treatment_null, size=n_control)

    sim_treatment = treatment_converted_r.mean()
    sim_control = control_converted_r.mean()

    p_diff = sim_treatment - sim_control

    p_diffs.append(p_diff)
